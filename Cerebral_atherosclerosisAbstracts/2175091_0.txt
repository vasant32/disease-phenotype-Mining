As many as 87 patients with different stages of discirculatory encephalopathy (DE) and 15 practically healthy persons (control group) underwent experimental and psychological studies of memory and attention. The data obtained were subjected to statistical processing. In the author's opinion, the studies made it possible to specify the character and extent of memory and attention disturbances at different stages of DE. The data obtained can be instrumental in routine clinical and expert work to diagnose DE stages, to control the treatment process.
