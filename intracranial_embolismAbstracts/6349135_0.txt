A review of the first 676 consecutive procedures of selective coronary arteriography performed from December 1st, 1979 to July 31st, 1982 in Innsbruck showed an incidence of cardiac complications of 0.89% and of non-cardiac complications of 0.74%. There was no cases of myocardial infarction and no fatality. There were no difference in significant complications during the first 32 months of experience in Innsbruck as compared with previous reports.
