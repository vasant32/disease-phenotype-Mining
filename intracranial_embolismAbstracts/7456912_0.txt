The degree of the participation of the right and the left lung in the respiration act was determined by the spirographic method with the use of the lateral test in 160 patients with various pathologies of the major cerebral vessels. In patients with pathological convolution of the vessels, stenoses, saccate and arteriovenous aneurisms in the internal carotid artery basin, the vital capacity of the right lung was found to be diminished (the diminution being statistically valid) regardless of the side of the cerebrovascular pathology.
