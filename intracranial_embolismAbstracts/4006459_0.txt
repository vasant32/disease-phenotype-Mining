The CT scan findings in 50 patients with cardiogenic cerebral emboli are reviewed. In 38 cases there was CT evidence of a nonhemorrhagic infarct and in 12 cases there were findings consistent with a hemorrhagic infarct. There was a single infarct in 40 cases and more than one lesion in 10 cases. In 6 of 9 cases in which the patients showed delayed neurological deterioration, repeat CT showed an increase in mass effect (4 cases) or hemorrhagic transformation of the infarct (2 cases).
