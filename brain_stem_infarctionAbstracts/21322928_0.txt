 OBJECTIVE: To study the etiology, clinical and pathological features, diagnose and treatment of Sturge-Weber syndrome. METHOD: The clinical data of 1 case of Sturge-Weber syndrome was analyzed retrospectively. RESULT: Sturge-Weber syndrome is sporadic neurocutaneous syndrome characterized with facial portwine stains, ocular vascular malformation. DSA can demonstrate excessive blood vessels on the leptomeninges. CONCLUSION: Sturge-Weber syndrome is a rare disease. DSA is helpful for the final diagnosis with its' clinical features.
